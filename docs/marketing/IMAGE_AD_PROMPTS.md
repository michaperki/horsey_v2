# Horsey Image Ad Prompts

Purpose: reusable image-generation prompts for Horsey advertising concepts. These are visual prompts, not final copy decks. Use them to generate campaign images, then place final text, logos, disclaimers, and app-store or compliance language in design tools where typography can be controlled.

Companion docs: `docs/PROJECT_SOUL.md`, `docs/DESIGN_REVIEW.md`, `docs/LOBBY_DESIGN_GAP.md`, `docs/SCOUTING_TRUST_NEXT_PASS.md`, `docs/LIVENESS_NEXT_PASS.md`.

## Brand Direction

Horsey is wagered chess with poker-floor energy. The image language should feel competitive, live, premium, and legible: chess board, clock, chips, felt table, escrow/pot cues, opponent scouting, rivalry, and instant settlement. The product should read as a real app and a live room, not a generic chess club or crypto casino.

Visual anchors:

- deep green felt, warm paper, ink-black pieces, gold money accents, small red urgency accents;
- physical chess pieces and poker chips in the same scene;
- mobile-first app surfaces, but with enough real-world table texture to feel tactile;
- live tension: clock pressure, hovering hand, opponent across the table, glowing pot rail;
- trust made visible through escrow, settlement ledger, profile/scout HUD, and clean UI cues.

Avoid:

- fake trust badges, KYC claims, fair-play scores, or legal claims unless backed by the product;
- implying live real-money availability before compliance and payments are approved;
- crypto/web3 aesthetics, coins raining down, neon casino clutter, roulette/cards/dice, slot-machine imagery;
- cartoon horse mascots, medieval fantasy chess art, smoky gambling dens, or generic stock-photo business people;
- unreadable UI text generated inside the image. Reserve important copy for layout after generation.

## Global Negative Prompt

Use this with any prompt when the generator supports negative prompts:

```text
no roulette, no slot machines, no playing cards, no cryptocurrency symbols, no neon cyberpunk casino, no cartoon mascot, no distorted chessboard, no impossible chess pieces, no messy unreadable app text, no fake legal badges, no fake verified badges, no excessive cash piles, no guns, no alcohol, no smoke-filled room
```

## Prompt Style Notes

- Prefer 4:5, 1:1, and 9:16 crops for social ads; use 16:9 for hero banners.
- Leave clean negative space for later copy placement.
- Ask for "blank UI labels" or "minimal abstract interface glyphs" rather than generated readable text.
- Keep stakes as visual chips or generic pot values unless the campaign has approved copy and disclaimers.

## Copy Pairing Ideas

Keep generated images mostly text-free. Pair with short external copy such as:

- `Pick a chip. Sit down.`
- `Chess with a pot on the rail.`
- `Scout the opponent before you sit.`
- `The board decides. Settlement follows.`
- `Fast tables. Real rivals. Clean settlement.`
- `Your move. Your clock. Your stake.`

Compliance-sensitive copy to avoid until approved:

- `Win real money`
- `Cash out instantly`
- `Guaranteed fair play`
- `Verified opponents`
- `Available in your state`
- `No risk`

## Production Checklist

Before using an image in public marketing:

- Confirm whether the campaign is for fake-money beta, sandbox testing, waitlist, or approved real-money launch.
- Add any required disclaimers outside the generated image.
- Ensure every visible product claim is backed by shipped or explicitly approved functionality.
- Verify the chessboard and pieces look plausible enough that chess players will not immediately reject the ad.
- Keep final typography in the design file, not baked into the generated image.
